Ingredients:

Water: 1 gallon
Kosher salt: 1 cup (adjust if using table salt-use 3/4 cup)
Brown sugar: 3/4 cup
Honey: 1/2 cup
Orange juice: 2 cups (freshly squeezed if possible)
Lemon juice: 1 cup
Apple cider: 2 cups
Fresh herbs: 2-3 sprigs each of thyme, rosemary, and sage
Spices: 1 tablespoon black peppercorns, 2 teaspoons crushed allspice, 2-3 bay leaves
Fruits: Sliced orange, lemon, and apple (1 of each)
Optional add-in: 1/2 cup of dried cranberries for an extra fruity touch.

Instructions:

In a large pot, combine water, salt, brown sugar, and honey. Heat gently until the salt and sugar are dissolved. Do not boil.
Remove from heat and add the orange juice, lemon juice, and apple cider. Stir to combine.
Add the fresh herbs, spices, and sliced fruits.
Cool the brine completely before using. You can speed this up by adding a couple of cups of ice.

Brining Process:

Place your turkey in a brining bag or a large, clean container. Make sure the turkey is completely thawed.
Pour the cooled brine over the turkey, ensuring it is fully submerged. You may need to weigh it down with a plate.
Refrigerate for 12-24 hours. For optimal results, aim for closer to 24 hours for a juicy and flavorful bird.
